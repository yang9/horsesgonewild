Wild Horses

Wild Horses are roaming free Everyday. There are about 33,000 wild horses running free in the United States. Descended from animals brought over by the Spanish in the 1500s, they roam throughout public lands in western states like Colorado, Nevada, Wyoming, and Montana.

Horses Stroll Everyday

Horses Stroll in water, on land, in meadows. It's a relaxing movement for them...sort of like jogging for people.

Fun is a Merry-Go-Round

Fun on Merry-Go-Rounds or RoundAbouts started in the 1800s, and are found in playgrounds. Carousels, also equipped with Horse figurines, are found in amusement parks..

Plowing Horse Work.

Plowing work used to be done by Horses or teams of Horses. They were replaced by machines that were versitle and powerful enough to work in farming environments (mud, hills, loose dirt. rocky dirt, roots). James Watt's steam engine ratings based on 550pounds lifted 1 foot in 1 second equals 1HP or 746Watts. 1ames Watt's steam engine was introduced into commercial production in 1776, but it wasn't until the mid-1800s that machines began to replace horses for plowing.

Horse and Wagon

Horse and Wagon was the main means of travel for America's expansion West. Whole Families moved West throughout the 1800s .

Quarter Horse

Quarter Horse. No not 1/4 of a Horse, but a special breed of Horse. Quarter Horses are powerfully swift runners over a quarter mile, and were frequently used by westerners in such races to settle challenges.

The Pony Express

The Pony Express carried mail & documents over a more than 2000 mile route from St. Joseph, Missour to San Fransisco, CA in 10 days or less. That was the fastest means of getting news & documents from coast to coast. The service lasted 19 months. Riders weighed 100-125lbs, road 10-15miles between each of the 200 relay stations before handing off their “mochila” (mailbag) to their relief rider after they'd covered 75-100miles. The service cost peaked at $5 per 1/2 oz., before ending at $1. The completion of the transcontinental telegraph dealt the Pony Express its deathblow.

War Horse

War Horses: Horses were first domesticated somewhere between 3500 & 2500 BCE. Horses in War is documented from chariot burials dated c. 2000 BCE. Horses were used in WW I anbd WW II.

Jumping Horses

Horse Jumping competitions are sophisticated games of skill. Horses have to learn to trust their rider. Horses have a 450 degree field of vision, but only a 65 degree field of vision with both eyes, and that limits their depth perception. They have a blindspot 3-4ft in front of their, face and another directly behind their head and along their backs as they look forward.

Rodeo Horses

Rodeo Horses do it all. They chase down calves, do stunt riding, are broncos and ride around the rodeo clowns.

Polo Pony

A Polo Pony is the term used for a horse used in the game of polo. They may be of any breed or combination of breeds, though many have a significant amount of Thoroughbred breeding. They very agile like ponies, require a great deal of training and ongoing physical condiioning. A Polo rider requires several horses during one match, this makes the sport an expensive pasttime.

Royal Horse

Royal Horses are perfect Status symbols, especially when they are adorned with Royal gaurds and fancy harnesses. Arabian stallions or Classical dressage breeds such as the Lipizzaner, Friesian, Andalusian, and Lusitano are excellent Royal Horses.

Trotter Horse

Trotter Horses are trained to move its legs forward in diagonal pairs (right front and left hind, then left front and right hind striking the ground simultaneously). The Trotters pull a two-wheeled cart, and their gaite resembles that of a runner stepping high with short, quick steps and bobbing up and down. Trotters must be continuously conditioned and are expensive to maintain. Status, sometimes, costs lots of MONEY.

Thoroughbred

The Thoroughbred as it is known today was developed in 17th- and 18th-century England, when native mares were crossbred with imported Oriental stallions of Arabian, Barb, and Turkoman breeding. The Thoroughbred is a horse breed best known for its use in horse racing. Secretariate, the 1973 Triple Crown Winner, was a uniquely magnificent Thoroughbred. He the powerful chest and speed of a Quarter Horse, and the strength and endurance of a Thouroghbred. He won all three legs of the 1973 Triple Crown going away from the pack. An example of his prowess, Secretariate won The Belmont Stakes by 25 lengths in a record 2minutes 24seconds.
